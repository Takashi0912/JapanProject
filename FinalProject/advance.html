Hokkaido - Sapporo Snow Festival

Held annually in Sapporo, Hokkaido, the Sapporo Snow Festival is one of Japan's most famous winter events. The festival features massive snow and ice sculptures, some towering several stories high, creating a winter wonderland in the city. Visitors can marvel at the intricate designs, enjoy snow slides and ice skating, and savor local winter foods. The festival, held in early February, attracts millions of visitors from around the world.

Fukushima - Ouchi-juku

Ouchi-juku is a historic post town in Fukushima Prefecture that has retained its Edo-period charm. The town features traditional thatched-roof buildings lining the main street, offering a picturesque journey back in time. Visitors can walk through the scenic streets, visit old-fashioned shops and inns, and try local specialties like negi soba, a noodle dish eaten with a leek instead of chopsticks.

Gifu - Yoro Park and Site of Reversible Destiny

Yoro Park in Gifu Prefecture is home to the whimsical and mind-bending Site of Reversible Destiny. This avant-garde park features disorienting architectural structures and landscapes designed to challenge your perception and senses. Visitors can explore the unusual pavilions, tilted walls, and labyrinthine paths, making it a unique and playful destination that offers a completely different experience from traditional.

Kanazawa - Kenrokuen Garden

Kenrokuen in Kanazawa is one of Japan's three most beautiful landscape gardens. Known for its perfect blend of the six essential garden attributes—spaciousness, seclusion, artifice, antiquity, watercourses, and panoramas—Kenrokuen offers stunning seasonal views year-round. Visitors can stroll through meticulously designed landscapes, including ponds, streams, tea houses, and flowering trees, experiencing the serene beauty of traditional Japanese gardening.

Kagawa - Naoshima

Naoshima, an island in Kagawa Prefecture, is a haven for contemporary art lovers. The island is dotted with art museums, installations, and sculptures, many designed by renowned architects like Tadao Ando. Highlights include the Chichu Art Museum, Benesse House, and numerous outdoor artworks. Naoshima's blend of art, architecture, and nature provides a unique and inspiring cultural experience.

Okinawa - Taketomi Island

Taketomi Island in Okinawa offers a glimpse into traditional Ryukyu culture. The island is known for its well-preserved traditional Okinawan houses with red-tiled roofs, white sandy roads, and lush gardens. Visitors can ride water buffalo-drawn carts, relax on the beautiful Kondoi Beach, and enjoy the laid-back island atmosphere. It's a perfect destination for experiencing Okinawa's unique cultural heritage and natural beauty.
